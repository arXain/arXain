Welcome to the arχain!

The first completely open access, distributed scientific journal.

Submit a paper »

arχain is a new kind of scientific journal.

arχain is an interface between the InterPlanetaryFileSystem (IPFS) and the Ethereum Blockchain, which defines a protocol for submitting, reviewing and revising academic papers in a completely open-access and distributed fashion.

Choosing to host your work on arχain has many benefits:

  • It is totally open access. Everyone can query every paper, since on the Blockchain, "GET" functions are free.
  • Get rapid feedback from the community as your peers upload their reviews of your paper.
  • Publication costs are low (~$50).
  • Papers are always accessible: there are no servers to "go down"... ever. On the Blockchain, we're all the sever!

Submitting a paper

To submit a paper to the arχain, you first initialize an Ethereum Smart Contract that will represent your paper on the Blockchain.

You can then point your contract to track a PDF that you uploaded to IPFS.

IPFS hashes your PDF, and your contract is intialized using your Ethereum wallet — so no-one can steal your work.

Reviewing a paper

Reviewing a paper works very similarly to submitting one. You initialize a Smart Contract that points to your review; a document on IPFS.

You can link your review to an existing paper on the arχain, and mark it as "needs more work" or "accepted". We'll handle the nitty-gritty.

Revising a paper

Peer-review is critical to modern science, and revising your paper in response to review comments helps to advance the field in general.

As with submissions and reviews, a revised paper is instantiated through a Smart Contract. However, you can point your revised paper to the same IPFS directory as your original one, and link it to that previous Smart Contract.

When the community reaches a concensus (an majority believe the paper should be accepted), that manuscript receives a special badge from arχain.

About Us

The first interation of the arχain was created at PennAppsXVII by 4 physics PhD candidates from the University of Pennsylvania. They continue to maintain and develop the platform to make it the best and most efficient journal it can be.
